# GRADE 4: ENGLISH

## 1. Nouns
- Name of a person, place, thing, or idea.
- Examples: girl, school, book, love
- **Proper nouns**: Names of people or places – always capitalized (e.g., Nairobi, John)

## 2. Verbs
- Action or state words.
- Examples: run, jump, eat, is, feel
- Present tense: I play
- Past tense: I played

## 3. Adjectives
- Words that describe nouns.
- Examples: red ball, happy child, tall tree

## 4. Adverbs
- Words that describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs.
- Examples: quickly, very, slowly, well

## 5. Sentences
- A group of words that tells a complete thought.
- Must start with a capital letter and end with a full stop (.), question mark (?), or exclamation mark (!)
- Example: I like apples.

## 6. Capital Letters
- Use for:
  - First word of a sentence
  - Names of people and places
  - The word "I"

## 7. Punctuation
- **Full stop (.)**: Ends a statement
- **Question mark (?)**: Ends a question
- **Exclamation mark (!)**: Shows strong feeling
- **Comma (,)**: Pauses in a list: apples, bananas, and oranges

## 8. Synonyms and Antonyms
- **Synonym**: Same meaning – big and large
- **Antonym**: Opposite meaning – hot and cold

## 9. Rhyming Words
- Words that sound the same at the end.
- Examples: cat, hat, mat; run, sun, fun

## 10. Reading and Writing
- Read daily to improve vocabulary
- Write short stories or paragraphs
- Always check spelling and punctuation
